# 🛡️ Bodyguard – Cyber Defense Hub

## Overview

Bodyguard is an intelligent cyber-defense platform designed to protect users from social media scams and online threats. The platform combines scam detection, phishing analysis, cybersecurity risk assessment, and AI-powered guidance into a single user-friendly solution.

The project was developed as part of a Design Thinking project on the topic:

**"Social Media Scams and User Vulnerability"**

---

## Problem Statement

With the rapid growth of social media platforms, users are increasingly exposed to cyber threats such as:

- Phishing attacks
- Fake investment schemes
- Relationship scams
- Deepfake fraud
- Identity theft
- Malicious links
- Social engineering attacks

Many users lack the awareness and tools required to identify and respond to these threats effectively.

---

## How Might We Statement

**How might we empower social media users to identify, prevent, and respond to online scams through an accessible, intelligent, and user-friendly cyber protection platform?**

---

## Solution

Bodyguard provides a comprehensive cybersecurity ecosystem built around four core pillars:

### 🔗 URL Fraud Radar
- Detects suspicious and malicious URLs.
- Identifies fake websites and typo-squatting domains.
- Provides risk scores and security recommendations.
- Helps users avoid dangerous links before clicking.

### 🎣 Phishing Analyzer
- Analyzes suspicious messages, emails, and social media DMs.
- Detects phishing indicators and social engineering tactics.
- Identifies urgency-based scams and credential harvesting attempts.
- Generates phishing risk assessments.

### 🛡️ Risk Audit System
- Evaluates user cybersecurity habits.
- Generates a personalized security score.
- Provides recommendations for improving online safety.
- Increases cybersecurity awareness.

### 🤖 AI Cyber Co-Pilot
- Acts as a virtual cybersecurity assistant.
- Provides guidance during cyber incidents.
- Assists users facing:
  - Account compromise
  - Phishing attacks
  - Blackmail attempts
  - Online harassment
  - Scam-related emergencies

## Research Methodology

### Primary Research

#### 1. Interviews
Conducted interviews with individuals who experienced social media scams to understand:

- Types of scams encountered
- User behavior during scams
- Emotional and financial impacts
- Awareness levels

#### 2. Google Form Surveys

Collected quantitative data regarding:

- Social media usage patterns
- Scam awareness
- Common vulnerabilities
- User expectations for protection tools

---

### Secondary Research

Research was conducted using multiple cybersecurity and fraud-related articles focusing on:

- AI-powered fraud prevention
- Voice phishing (Vishing)
- Deepfake scams
- Financial fraud
- Social engineering techniques

## Prototype Testing
